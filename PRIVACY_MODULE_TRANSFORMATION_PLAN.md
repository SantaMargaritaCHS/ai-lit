## 🔍 PHASE 1: RESEARCH DATA (2025)

### Social Media Terms & Conditions Research

#### Instagram (Meta)
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Estimated Length:** ~17,000 words
**Reading Time:** ~68 minutes
**Key Findings:**
- [ ] Can use user data for AI training (NO opt-out in US)
- [ ] Mandatory binding arbitration (can't sue or join class action)
- [ ] Collects extensive data for advertising targeting
- [ ] UK/EU users can opt out of AI training, US users cannot
- [ ] Data shared across Meta companies (Facebook, WhatsApp)

**Shocking Clauses to Highlight:**
```
- "We can use your photos to train AI"
- "We share data with 1,000+ partners"
- "You grant us royalty-free license to your content"
```

#### TikTok
**Data Collection:** Biometric identifiers (faceprints, voiceprints)
**Estimated Length:** ~12,000 words
**Reading Time:** ~48 minutes
**Key Findings:**
- [ ] Collects "faceprints and voiceprints" from user content since 2021
- [ ] Used for filters, content moderation, demographic classification
- [ ] Paid $92M settlement for Illinois Biometric Privacy Act violations
- [ ] Vague policy - doesn't specify which state laws it follows
- [ ] Most US states don't require consent notifications

**Shocking Clauses:**
```
- "May collect biometric identifiers and biometric information"
- "Faceprints and voiceprints from your content"
- "For special video effects, content moderation, demographic classification"
```

#### Snapchat (My AI)
**Estimated Length:** ~15,000 words
**Reading Time:** ~60 minutes
**Key Findings:**
- [ ] My AI conversations stored INDEFINITELY (unlike friend chats)
- [ ] Content used to improve products and personalize ads
- [ ] Targeted ads based on conversation context over time
- [ ] All queries can be analyzed by Snap's team
- [ ] UK ICO investigated in 2023, compliance reached 2024

**Shocking Clauses:**
```
- "We retain content with My AI until you delete it manually"
- "Your My AI conversations personalize ads"
- "All queries can be analyzed by our team"
```

#### Character.AI
**Policy Update:** August 27, 2025
**Estimated Length:** ~8,000 words
**Reading Time:** ~32 minutes
**Key Findings:**
- [ ] Uses ALL conversations to train AI models
- [ ] Employees access chats to train language models
- [ ] Can sell user conversations
- [ ] Ad targeting based on conversations
- [ ] NO opt-out mechanism (must delete account)
- [ ] NO end-to-end encryption
- [ ] Mandatory arbitration (no class actions)

**Shocking Clauses:**
```
- "We use chat data to train our AI/ML models"
- "Character AI employees use your data to train models"
- "We may sell user conversations"
- "Deleting your account is the only way to prevent future data use"
```

### AI Tools Privacy Comparison (2025)

#### HIGH RISK (Trains on Data):
- [ ] **ChatGPT Free/Plus** - ALL conversations used for training, no opt-out
- [ ] **Character.AI** - ALL conversations, employee access, can sell data
- [ ] **Snapchat My AI** - Stored indefinitely, personalizes ads
- [ ] **Replika** - Conversations stored, used for model improvement

#### MEDIUM RISK:
- [ ] **ChatGPT Pro** - Can disable training in Settings → Data Controls (not default)
- [ ] **Google Gemini** - Deep Google integration, cross-service data sharing
- [ ] **Microsoft Copilot** - Consumer version trains, Enterprise/Education has protections
- [ ] **Perplexity AI** - Search queries logged, third-party sharing

#### LOWER RISK:
- [ ] **Claude.ai** - Does NOT train on conversations by default (best privacy)
- [ ] **DuckDuckGo AI Chat** - No accounts, no training, privacy-focused
- [ ] **Local AI apps** - Data stays on device
